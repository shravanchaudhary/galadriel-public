# Content Strategy: Metrics & Analytics Tracking

Tracking performance metrics is vital for evaluating content quality, optimizing publishing cadence, and demonstrating return on investment (ROI). Data-driven creators use analytics to continuously refine their strategy.

## Key Content Metrics & Interpretations

### 1. Impressions (Reach)
*   **What it measures:** The number of times your post was rendered on a screen.
*   **Interpretation:** High impressions signal that your hook and visual format successfully aligned with the feed algorithm's distribution criteria.

### 2. Engagement Rate
*   **Calculation:** `(Reactions + Comments + Reposts + Clicks) / Impressions`.
*   **Benchmarks:**
    *   *Below 2%:* Low engagement. The content or format did not resonate with the audience.
    *   *2% to 5%:* Healthy, average engagement.
    *   *Over 5%:* High engagement. The post sparked strong resonance or discussion.

### 3. Click-Through Rate (CTR)
*   **Calculation:** `Clicks on Link / Impressions` (only applicable for posts with external URLs).
*   **Benchmark:** A healthy CTR on LinkedIn ranges from **1% to 3%**.

### 4. Share/Repost Rate
*   **Calculation:** `Shares / Impressions`.
*   **Interpretation:** High share rates signal that your content was viewed as extremely valuable, educational, or highly representative of the audience's professional identity.

## Sourcing Inbound Intent Signals

Beyond content performance, creators must track inbound engagement metrics to capture high-intent leads:

1.  **Profile Viewers:**
    *   *The Signal:* Who viewed your profile over the past 90 days.
    *   *Strategic Playbook:* If a profile viewer matches your target buyer/candidate persona, flag them immediately. They are researching you; send a personalized, warm outreach within 24 hours.
2.  **Post Demographics:**
    *   Audit the Job Titles, Companies, and Locations of your post impressions. If your content is reaching the wrong audience (e.g., students instead of CTOs), adjust your keyword usage and topic complexity.
3.  **Active Follower Growth:**
    *   Track net follower growth weekly. Spikes in follower growth should be correlated with specific high-performing posts or newsletter launches.
