# Content Strategy: Post Formats and Cadence

Optimizing post formats and maintaining a consistent publishing schedule are essential for maximizing organic reach and engagement on the LinkedIn feed. Different formats serve different psychological needs of the audience.

## Optimizing Content Formats

Based on feed ranking and user engagement behaviors, here is how to execute different post formats:

### 1. PDF Carousels (Document Posts)
*   **Organic Weight:** **Highest organic distribution**. The feed algorithm rewards the high dwell time spent clicking through pages.
*   **Best Practices:**
    *   Design for mobile (1:1 square or 4:5 vertical aspect ratio).
    *   Limit to 7-10 slides with minimal, high-impact text.
    *   Use bold, clear headers and a visual progress indicator.

### 2. Text-Only and Single-Image Posts
*   **Organic Weight:** Medium to High. Highly reliable for personal storytelling and quick updates.
*   **Best Practices:**
    *   Write compelling hooks in the first 3 lines before the "See more" truncation (~200-300 characters).
    *   Use spacing, bullet points, and short sentences to optimize readability.

### 3. Video Posts
*   **Organic Weight:** Medium.
*   **Best Practices:**
    *   Keep videos short (under 2 minutes) and high-tempo.
    *   **Always add subtitles/closed captions**. Over 80% of members consume video on mute while scrolling.

### 4. Polls
*   **Organic Weight:** High (when controversial or highly relatable), but drives low conversion.
*   **Best Practices:**
    *   Use to trigger early engagement and gather market insights.
    *   Follow up with participants via direct messaging to build warm relationships.

## Sourcing a Consistent Publishing Cadence

Consistency builds brand recognition and signals professional dedication.

### Recommended Posting Schedule
*   **Frequency:** **3 to 4 times per week**. Avoid posting more than once a day, as subsequent posts compete with each other and cannibalize feed distribution.
*   **Best Days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday.
*   **Best Times:** Between 8 AM and 10 AM local time of your target audience (catching them during morning commutes or early work sessions).

### Content Sourcing & Batching
*   **Batching Strategy:** Dedicate one block of 2-3 hours per week to research, outline, and draft all posts for the upcoming week. This prevents daily stress and guarantees quality.
*   **Template Library:** Maintain a repository of successful formatting structures (hooks, bullet styles, CTAs) that can be easily repurposed.
