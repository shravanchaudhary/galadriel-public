# Content Strategy: Copywriting Hooks

A hook is the first 2-3 lines of a LinkedIn post displayed before the "See more" truncation. It is the single most critical copywriting element, as it determines whether a user stops scrolling and engages with your post.

```
+-------------------------------------------------------------+
|                      POST TRUNCATION                        |
+-------------------------------------------------------------+
|                                                             |
|   Line 1: High-impact statement or controversial claim      |
|   Line 2: Supporting intrigue or contrasting observation    |
|   Line 3: Open loop or build-up                             |
|                                                             |
|   [See more] <--- Truncation Point (Goal: Drive this click)  |
|                                                             |
+-------------------------------------------------------------+
```

## Anatomy of a High-Converting Hook

1.  **Stop the Scroll:** Start with a bold, surprising, or counterintuitive statement.
2.  **Create Curiosity:** Open a cognitive gap or "loop" that can only be resolved by reading the rest of the post.
3.  **Establish Relevance:** Clearly signal who this post is for and what value they will gain from reading it.

---

## 4 Hook Archetypes (With Templates)

### 1. The Counterintuitive Statement
Challenging industry common sense triggers intrigue and debate.
*   **Template:**
    *   `[Common practice] is actually killing your [desired outcome]. Here's why:`
    *   `Most [professionals] think they need [A] to succeed. They are wrong. It's actually [B].`
*   **Example:**
    *   *"Cold outbound is dead. Senders embracing mass spam see an abysmal 1% response rate. Here's what top performers do instead:"*

### 2. The Metric-Driven Success Story
Providing hard numbers builds immediate credibility and interest.
*   **Template:**
    *   `How we helped [Type of company] achieve [Metric] in just [Timeframe] (without [Pain Point]):`
    *   `I analyzed [X] of the best [Y]. Here are the [Z] key takeaways you can apply today:`
*   **Example:**
    *   *"We spent 2022 analyzing every Sales Navigator InMail sent. Here are the 6 major data-backed lessons that will double your response rates:"*

### 3. The Personal Vulnerability Lesson
Sharing personal struggles or mistakes builds deep human connection.
*   **Template:**
    *   `I spent [X] years doing [Y] the wrong way. Here are the [Z] mistakes I wish I'd avoided:`
    *   `The hardest lesson I learned as a [Role] came from losing our [biggest client/opportunity]:`
*   **Example:**
    *   *"I lost our biggest deal last year because our single champion left the company. That painful mistake taught me the power of multithreading. Here is our new playbook:"*

### 4. The Value-First Curation
Offering a curated package of high-value resources drives massive saves and reposts.
*   **Template:**
    *   `I compiled the ultimate checklist for [Professional Goal]. Here's the complete breakdown:`
    *   `Stop wasting time on [X]. Here are [Y] free tools that will save you 10+ hours a week:`
*   **Example:**
    *   *"Don't feed your agent a single LinkedIn guide. Feed it a structured LinkedIn Memory Corpus. Here is our recommended memory architecture:"*
