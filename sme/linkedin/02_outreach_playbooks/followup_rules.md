# Outreach Playbook: Follow-up Rules & Cadence

A structured follow-up cadence is what turns a single, ignored message into a successful conversation. B2B professionals are busy; follow-ups keep your name at the top of their inbox, but they must be timed and structured to avoid feeling spammy.

## The Optimal 4-Step Follow-Up Cadence

```
[ Day 1: Initial Pitch ]  <--- Concise, highly personalized, soft CTA
         |
         v (Wait 3-4 days)
[ Day 4: Value Bump ]     <--- Share a relevant case study, PDF, or blog post
         |
         v (Wait 5-7 days)
[ Day 10: Soft Prompt ]   <--- Quick question touching on their specific role/needs
         |
         v (Wait 7-10 days)
[ Day 20: Break-Up ]      <--- Final soft close, leaving the door open for future contact
```

### Step 1: The Initial Outreach (Day 1)
*   **Message Type:** InMail or Connection Request Note.
*   **Goal:** Establish relevance, mention uncommon commonalities, and make a soft, non-invasive call to action.

### Step 2: The Value Bump (Day 4)
*   **Wait Time:** 3 to 4 business days.
*   **Strategy:** Never follow up with "Just checking in" or "Wanted to bump this." Always offer immediate value.
*   **Template:**
    ```text
    "Hi [Name], thought you might find this interesting—we recently published a guide on how [Similar Company] reduced [Pain Point] by 30%. No need to reply, just thought of your team: [Link]"
    ```

### Step 3: The Soft Prompt (Day 10)
*   **Wait Time:** 5 to 7 business days.
*   **Strategy:** Ask a low-friction question relating to their current priorities.
*   **Template:**
    ```text
    "Hi [Name], quick question—is managing [Pain Point] a priority for your team in Q3, or are you currently focused elsewhere? Always open to exchanging ideas."
    ```

### Step 4: The Break-Up Message (Day 20)
*   **Wait Time:** 7 to 10 business days.
*   **Strategy:** Politely close the thread, removing pressure and leaving the door open. This frequently triggers replies from prospects who were simply too busy.
*   **Template:**
    ```text
    "Hi [Name], looks like the timing isn't right for this conversation. Totally understand! I'll stop reaching out. Feel free to connect down the line if [Topic] ever becomes a focus. Wish you the best! [My Name]"
    ```

---

## Strategic Rules for Follow-Ups

1.  **Integrate Social Touchpoints:**
    *   Between messages, view their profile or leave a thoughtful comment on their recent post. This increases your name recognition in their notification feed without adding messages to their inbox.
2.  **Stop Cadence on Reply:**
    *   The moment a prospect replies (even a negative response), **immediately halt the automated cadence**. Transition to a manual, highly conversational response.
3.  **Strict Timing Compliance:**
    *   Never send follow-up messages on weekends. Ensure follow-ups land on workdays (Monday through Thursday) between 9 AM and 4 PM local time.
