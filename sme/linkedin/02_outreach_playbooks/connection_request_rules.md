# Outreach Playbook: Connection Request Rules & Best Practices

To grow a high-quality professional network while keeping the account safe and in good standing, connection requests must be executed following strict rules, boundaries, and best practices.

## Structural Constraints & Limits

1.  **Character Limits:**
    *   **Basic / Free Members:** Personalized connection notes are limited to exactly **200 characters**. They can only add notes to **3 connection requests per month**.
    *   **Premium / Sales Navigator Members:** Personalized connection notes are limited to **300 characters** (platform updates may vary, but 300 characters is the standard benchmark). There are **no monthly limits** on personalizing notes.
2.  **Weekly Limits:**
    *   LinkedIn enforces a dynamic weekly connection request limit. This typically ranges from **100 to 200 requests per week**, depending on:
        *   Account age and subscription level (Premium/Sales Navigator accounts have higher tolerances).
        *   Acceptance rate (low acceptance rates result in lower limits).
        *   The percentage of requests marked as "I don't know this person" by recipients.

## Crafting a Compliant 200 & 300 Character Note

Since characters are highly limited, notes must avoid fluff and get straight to relevance.

### Template 1: Shared Group / Content (Under 200 Characters - For Free Members)
```text
"Hi [Name], loved your comment in [Group Name] about [Topic]! I'm a [My Role] and would love to connect to follow your updates. Cheers, [My Name]"
```
*(Character count: ~150 chars)*

### Template 2: Job Transition (Under 300 Characters - For Premium Members)
```text
"Hi [Name], congratulations on your new role at [Company]! Transitioning can be a whirlwind, but I've been following your company's growth in [Industry] and would love to connect and keep in touch. Best, [My Name]"
```
*(Character count: ~230 chars)*

## Behavioral Guidelines for High Acceptance Rates

1.  **Never Pitch in the Connection Request:** A connection invitation is a digital handshake, not a sales pitch. Pitching immediately results in ignored requests and potential "spam" markings.
2.  **Target Active Members:**
    *   Filter prospects who have **posted on LinkedIn in the last 30 days** (**45% more likely to accept**).
3.  **Target Career Milestones:**
    *   Target prospects who have started a new job within the last 90 days (**62% more likely to accept**) or a new role within the same company (**51% more likely to accept**).
4.  **Pending Request Cleanup:**
    *   If you have a backlog of over 500 pending sent requests, withdraw the oldest ones (older than 2-3 weeks) to maintain a healthy account reputation and prevent weekly restriction flags.
5.  **User-in-the-Loop Safe Zones:**
    *   To prevent automation triggers, an agent should draft the note, but let the user review and click "Send". This keeps a natural browsing cadence.
