# User Intent: Hiring

The "Hiring" intent represents recruiters, human resources professionals, and team leaders looking to source, evaluate, and acquire high-quality talent for their organizations.

## Behavioral Signals of a Hiring Professional

1.  **Direct Signaling:**
    *   Posting job openings via LinkedIn Jobs.
    *   Adding the purple `#Hiring` frame to their profile photo.
    *   Publishing "We're hiring" posts detailing open roles, company culture, and benefits.
2.  **Platform Sourcing Behaviors:**
    *   Using LinkedIn Recruiter to run structured candidate searches.
    *   Frequently viewing profiles of prospective candidates.
    *   Reaching out to active ("Open to Work") or passive talent via InMail.

## Sourcing & Sourcing Playbooks

### 1. Active vs. Passive Talent
*   **Active Candidates:** Currently looking, signaled by the `#OpenToWork` badge. High response rates, short hiring timelines, but highly competitive.
*   **Passive Candidates:** Not actively looking but open to the right opportunity. Lower response rates, but represent a massive, high-quality talent pool. Requires hyper-customized outreach emphasizing career growth, work-life balance, or technological challenges.

### 2.Sourcing Funnel for Sourcing
1.  **Define Persona:** Build a strict search string utilizing Boolean logic (e.g., `"Software Engineer" AND (Python OR Go) AND "Distributed Systems"`).
2.  **Target Warm Candidates:**
    *   Prioritize candidates who follow your Company Page (**270% more likely to respond**).
    *   Target "Recommended Matches" (**35% higher acceptance rate**).
    *   Leverage candidates with mutual connections.
3.  **Outreach Rules:**
    *   **Keep it short:** Under 400 characters gets **22% above average** responses.
    *   **Personalize individually:** Individually tailored messages see a **15% lift** in responses.
    *   **Schedule timing:** Send on Monday mornings; strictly **avoid Fridays and Saturdays**.
