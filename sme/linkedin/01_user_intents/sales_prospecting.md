# User Intent: Sales Prospecting

The "Sales Prospecting" intent represents B2B sales development representatives (SDRs), account executives (AEs), and business development leaders utilizing LinkedIn to identify target accounts, source decision-makers, establish relationships, and book sales opportunities.

## Deep Sales Philosophy

Modern sales has evolved away from transactional mass spam to **Deep Sales**. Deep sales prioritizes in-depth buyer research, warm relationship paths, intent data, and custom value delivery over quantity-based cold outreach.

### 1. Account Selection & Prioritization
*   **Whitespace & Sizing:** Filter accounts using `Annual Revenue`, `Company Headcount`, or `Department Headcount` to assess opportunity size.
*   **Growth Signals:** Filter by `Headcount Growth`, `Department Headcount Growth`, or `Funding Event` to identify accounts with budget.
*   **Buyer Intent Signals:** Leverage Sales Navigator's `Buyer Intent` filter to find accounts that have recently viewed your profile, engaged with your company page, or visited your website.

### 2. Sourcing Decision-Makers & Mapping
*   **Persona Sourcing:** Define standard buyer personas in Sales Navigator (e.g., VP of Finance, Chief Security Officer).
*   **Multithreading Strategy:** Never rely on a single champion. Connectedness on LinkedIn to at least **4 people at an account increases close rates by 16%**.
*   **Account Mapping:** Categorize saved leads into tiers:
    *   *Tier 1 (Executive):* Decision-Makers & Budget Holders.
    *   *Tier 2 (Management):* Key Influencers & Project Leads.
    *   *Tier 3 (Individual):* Champions & Day-to-day Users.

### 3. Sourcing Warm Paths and Personalized Outreach
*   **Relationship Explorer:** Use this feature to identify hidden paths in, such as shared past employers, mutual connections, or shared groups.
*   **Timing Signals:** Reach out shortly after a target buyer starts a new role. New hires are **65% more likely to accept an InMail** and are eager to make an impact.
*   **Personalization rules:** Avoid cold pitches. Reference "uncommon commonalities" (mutual experiences or rare interests) to build rapid trust.
