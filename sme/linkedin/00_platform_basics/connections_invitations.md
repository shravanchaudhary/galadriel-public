# Connecting & Invitations

Connecting on LinkedIn is the mechanism of converting 2nd or 3rd-degree prospects into 1st-degree connections. Managing invitations with precision is vital for network expansion and account safety.

## invitation Mechanics

### Personalized Invitations
Personalizing connection requests heavily increases the likelihood of acceptance. It sets a foundation of relevance and professional courtesy.

*   **Basic / Free Members:**
    *   Can add personalized messages to up to **3 connection requests per month**.
    *   Character limit is strictly **200 characters** for the note.
*   **Premium / Sales Navigator Members:**
    *   Can send **unlimited personalized invitations** (though global weekly connection limits still apply).
    *   Character limit is **300 characters** (varies slightly by platform updates, but 300 is the standard benchmark).

### Guidelines for Personalized Notes
1.  **Introduce Context:** Explain how you found them (e.g., "Came across your post on...", "Saw your profile in [Group]").
2.  **Establish Common Ground:** Mention mutual connections, shared education, or overlapping industries.
3.  **Specify Value / Intent:** Clearly but gently state why you'd like to connect without initiating a sales pitch.
4.  **Keep it Brief:** Shorter, punchy notes stand out.

## invitation Safety & Restrictions

To protect the platform from spam and automated bots, LinkedIn enforces strict invitation limits and monitors behavioral patterns:

### 1. Weekly Connection Limits
*   LinkedIn enforces a dynamic limit of approximately **100 to 200 connection requests per week** (depending on the account's historical acceptance rate, profile strength, and subscription tier).
*   Exceeding this limit triggers an "Out of Invitations" warning.

### 2. Spam-Marked and Pending Invites
*   If too many recipients click "I don't know this person" or "Report Spam" upon receiving your invite, LinkedIn will restrict your ability to send invitations.
*   **Pending Invites Clean-up:** Keep pending sent invites low. If you have more than 500-1000 pending invites that have been ignored for over 2-3 weeks, withdraw them to protect your account health.

### 3. Rate-Limiting & Automation Alerts
*   Sending invitations rapidly in a short window (e.g., clicking 'Connect' multiple times in seconds) triggers CAPTCHAs or temporary account restrictions.
*   A user-in-the-loop copilot architecture is the only way to safeguard against these bans, avoiding automated browser-level click loops.
