# Connections & The Network Graph

LinkedIn is structured as a professional network graph where members are nodes and connections are edges. The degree of separation between nodes determines messaging capabilities, profile visibility, and retrieval prioritization in search results.

```
       [ 1st-Degree ]  <--- Immediate Connections (Direct Chat, High Feed Priority)
             |
             v
       [ 2nd-Degree ]  <--- Shared Connections (Connect with Personal Note, InMail Option)
             |
             v
       [ 3rd-Degree ]  <--- Out-of-Network (InMail Only, Limited Profile Visibility)
```

## Graph Degrees & Interaction Rules

### 1st-Degree Connections
*   **Definition:** People you are directly connected to (you accepted their invitation or they accepted yours).
*   **Capabilities:**
    *   Direct messaging for free (no InMail credits required).
    *   Direct access to contact info (email/phone if made visible by the user).
    *   Highest priority in the feed algorithm (network activities frequently surface on your home feed).
    *   Visible in "My Network" lists.

### 2nd-Degree Connections
*   **Definition:** People who are connected to your 1st-degree connections (i.e., you have mutual connections).
*   **Capabilities:**
    *   Can send connection requests with personalized messages.
    *   Can send InMails if you have a Premium/Sales Navigator subscription.
    *   Profile details are mostly visible (headline, about, shared experiences, mutual connections).

### 3rd-Degree Connections
*   **Definition:** People connected to your 2nd-degree connections (no direct mutual connection).
*   **Capabilities:**
    *   If their full name is displayed: Can send a connection request (but may have restricted options compared to 2nd-degree).
    *   If only the first letter of their last name is displayed: Must send an InMail to reach out.
    *   Limited profile visibility depending on their private privacy settings.

### Followers vs. Connections
*   **Connections:** A two-way relationship. Both members see each other's updates in their feeds. Limits apply (maximum 30,000 1st-degree connections).
*   **Followers:** A one-way relationship. The follower sees the followed member's public updates in their feed, but not vice-versa. There is no limit on the number of followers.
*   **Strategic Use:** Turning on "Creator Mode" makes "Follow" the default primary action on the profile. This allows unlimited audience scale while protecting the 30,000 connection limit.
